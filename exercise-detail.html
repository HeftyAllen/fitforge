Barbell Bench Press

Instructions

1

Setup

Lie flat on a bench with your feet firmly on the ground. Your eyes should be directly under the barbell. Grip the bar slightly wider than shoulder-width apart.

2

Unrack the Bar

Lift the bar off the rack and hold it directly above your chest with arms fully extended. Keep your wrists straight and elbows tucked slightly.

3

Lower the Bar

Slowly lower the bar to your mid-chest while keeping your elbows at about a 45-degree angle from your body. Maintain control throughout the movement.

4

Press Up

Drive the bar back up to the starting position by pushing through your chest and triceps. Exhale during the upward motion and maintain a stable base.

Tips & Common Mistakes

Do: Keep Shoulders Back

Retract your shoulder blades throughout the movement to protect your shoulders and engage the chest muscles properly.

Don't: Bounce the Bar

Avoid bouncing the bar off your chest as this can cause injury and reduces muscle engagement. Control the descent.

Do: Use a Spotter

Always use a spotter when lifting heavy weights to ensure safety and proper form throughout your sets.

Don't: Flare Elbows

Avoid letting your elbows flare out to the sides as this puts excessive stress on the shoulder joints.
